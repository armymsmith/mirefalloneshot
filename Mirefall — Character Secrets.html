⚰Oryn Ashvale
Grave Cleric · Half-Elf
— fold here · read alone —
☩ Your Secret ☩

You know the name Mirefall. Your grandmother spoke it in her sleep for the last decade of her life — always the same word, always with the same expression. Not fear. Guilt.

The Ashvale family was one of six families feuding with the church over land the year of the flood. Your grandmother was eight years old. She was on the hillside. She saw everything.

She never told you what she saw. But she made you promise, on her deathbed, to go to Mirefall if you ever heard the bell.

You heard it three weeks ago. You don't know how.

Ask yourself — Does your character come here to make something right, or to make sure no one finds out what their family did?

🌑Vesper Crane
Gloom Stalker Ranger · Human
— fold here · read alone —
☩ Your Secret ☩

Your patrol partner, Dren, went missing on Mirefall Lake seven days ago. He was one of two fishermen who rowed out to investigate the bell. Neither came back.

You and Dren weren't just partners. You were the closest thing either of you had to family. You've been tracking his last known movements for a week and the trail ends at the dockhouse.

You are not here for the reward. You are here because Dren is still out there. You can feel it — the same instinct that's kept you alive in the dark for years. He's alive. Something is keeping him that way on purpose.

What it wants in return, you don't know yet.

Ask yourself — When you find out what's down there, will you be able to leave without Dren — even if he asks you to?

Vengeance Paladin · Tiefling
— fold here · read alone —
☩ Your Secret ☩

You've had the same dream for six months. You're underwater. It's dark and very cold. Something is ringing — enormous, close, like you're inside the bell itself. And a voice says the same thing every time, low and deliberate:

"Bring it back."

Tucked in the lining of your pack is a small piece of cast iron — old, corroded, curved. You found it on a road three towns back with no memory of picking it up. A blacksmith told you it was bell metal. Very old bell metal.

You don't know what it is. But the closer you get to Mirefall, the warmer it gets.

Ask yourself — The voice said bring it back. What happens when you do — and do you have a choice?

💀Thessaly Vorne
Necromancer Wizard · Half-Elf
— fold here · read alone —
☩ Your Secret ☩

The Vorne family was one of six families involved in the land dispute with Father Voss the year of the flood. You didn't know this — your family never spoke of Mirefall. But your research into your own genealogy brought you here, and what you found in the old records made you feel sick.

Your ancestor signed the dispute paperwork. Withdrew from it last-minute. Then — according to a single faded line in a parish record you found — tried to send warning to the chapel and was stopped by the other families.

Your ancestor tried to save him. Was prevented. And has never been part of the story anyone tells.

You are not sure if you came here to exonerate your family or to finally understand why the dead have always spoken so easily to you.

Ask yourself — If Father Voss knows your name — your family name — what do you hope he says?

👻Ash
Phantom Rogue · Halfling
— fold here · read alone —
☩ Your Secret ☩

You don't remember anything before six years ago. You woke up on a riverbank with no name, no past, and no explanation. You named yourself Ash because it felt right — like something that used to be something else.

You've always been able to hear things others can't. Not voices exactly. More like the echo of feelings that got left somewhere. Grief. Guilt. The specific weight of something unfinished.

The closer you get to Mirefall, the louder it gets. And last night you dreamed of standing in cold water up to your chest, ringing a bell you couldn't stop ringing, watching people on a hillside watch you drown.

You don't know whose memory that is.

Ask yourself — What if you didn't just survive something at that river? What if part of you didn't survive at all?

✉Brother Mael
Shadow Monk · Elf
— fold here · read alone —
☩ Your Secret ☩

Your abbot gave you a sealed letter and told you to deliver it to Father Aldric Voss at Mirefall Lake. You were told not to open it. You were told not to ask questions. You were told this was a matter of great spiritual importance.

You have since learned that Father Voss drowned a century ago.

The letter is still sealed. You have not opened it. But you've held it enough times to notice it has no weight. Paper and wax should have weight. This has none. And sometimes, in the deepest part of your meditation, you think you can hear something moving inside it.

You are beginning to suspect your monastery has been sending monks to this lake for a very long time. None of the ones before you came back.

Ask yourself — When the time comes — and it will — do you deliver the letter? Do you open it? Or do you let it sink?

🎶Isolde Mourne
College of Spirits Bard · Human
— fold here · read alone —
☩ Your Secret ☩

Your sister Lira went to Mirefall two years ago chasing a rumor about a haunted lake and never came home. The official story is that she drowned. You don't believe it — Lira was a stronger swimmer than anyone you've ever known, and she was careful, and she knew what she was doing.

What you haven't told anyone: three months after she disappeared, you woke in the night to the sound of her voice. Just two words. He's waiting.

You've been a collector of ghost stories your whole life. You have spoken to dozens of the dead through your music. But you have never been able to reach Lira — not once, not even a whisper.

Something at that lake is keeping her from you. You intend to find out what.

Ask yourself — If you reach Lira and she tells you to leave — that she chose to stay — can you accept that?

⚒Calder Thorne
Rune Knight Fighter · Dwarf
— fold here · read alone —
☩ Your Secret ☩

Three weeks ago you woke up with marks on your skin. Both forearms, shoulder to wrist — runes you don't recognize, in no script you've ever studied, etched as if they'd always been there. They don't hurt. They don't fade. And when you are near water, they glow faintly blue.

You haven't told anyone. You've been covering them with your armor.

A scholar you consulted in secret recognized one symbol — a binding ward, very old, used in drowned-god rituals to mark someone as a vessel. She didn't know for what. She suggested, carefully, that you not go near any large bodies of water.

You came to Mirefall anyway because you need answers more than you need safety.

Ask yourself — The runes appeared the same night the bell started ringing. What does Father Voss want with you specifically?
