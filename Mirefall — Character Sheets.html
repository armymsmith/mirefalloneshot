BACKSTORY
Oryn grew up in a family that never spoke of the past. Their grandmother, a sharp-eyed woman named Hessa, spent her final years sleeping restlessly and waking from dreams she refused to describe. She died with an expression that Oryn could only call guilt. When Oryn entered the clergy of the Grave domain — drawn to the idea that the dead deserve honesty even when the living won't provide it — they began researching their family line. The trail led to a village called Mirefall and a land dispute involving a priest named Aldric Voss. What Oryn found in the church records made them feel sick: House Ashvale had watched a man drown from a hillside and said nothing. Oryn came to Mirefall with no plan except to find out if there is any way to make something like that right — and whether forgiveness can be offered on behalf of people who never asked for it.
PERSONALITY

FLAW

Ask yourself: do you come here to make something right, or to make sure no one finds out what your family did?

Weathered, sharp, deliberate. Speaks carefully. Carries herself like someone braced for a reckoning that's sixty years overdue. She chose this moment — the reward notice was an act of exhausted surrender, not confidence.
What She Knows
Everything. She knows the six families watched Voss drown. She knows her own grandmother was on the hillside. She has never told anyone. The letter under her floorboard is the only record.
How She Breaks
Finding Marta's letter first, then confronting her with it, causes her to break down completely. She doesn't ask for forgiveness — she asks if it's too late for one. Roleplaying opportunity. Does not reward violence.
Reward
50 gp as posted. Also offers her late husband's +1 shortsword if the party resolves Voss peacefully. Withholds the extra reward if the party forces a violent resolution.
FATHER ALDRIC VOSS — Drowned Revenant, CR 6
AC 13 (natural armor)  ·  HP 136 (16d8 + 64)  ·  Speed 30 ft, swim 30 ft
STR 18 (+4)   DEX 11 (+0)   CON 18 (+4)   INT 13 (+1)   WIS 16 (+3)   CHA 16 (+3)
Saving Throws STR +7, CON +7, WIS +6, CHA +6
Damage Immunities poison, necrotic  ·  Condition Immunities charmed, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned, stunned
Senses Darkvision 60 ft., Truesight 30 ft.  ·  Languages Common, Celestial
Regeneration
Regains 10 HP at the start of his turn unless he took radiant damage or was reduced to 0 HP. If at 0 HP, he rises again in 1 hour at full HP — unless given rest by completing the ritual or acknowledging the truth.
The Bell's Pull (Aura, 30 ft.)
Creatures within 30 ft. must make a DC 14 WIS save at the start of their turn or be pulled 10 ft. toward Voss. On a failed save by 5+, the creature is also stunned until the end of their next turn.
Multiattack
Voss makes two Drowning Grip attacks.
Drowning Grip. Melee Weapon Attack
+7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 11 (2d6+4) bludgeoning + 9 (2d8) necrotic. Target must succeed on a DC 14 CON save or lose the ability to speak until the end of their next turn.
The Bell Tolls (Recharge 5–6)
Voss rings his spectral bell. All creatures within 60 ft. must make a DC 14 CON save. On a failure: 4d10 thunder damage, deafened for 1 minute. On a success: half damage, not deafened.
Trance (Legendary Action, 1/round)
One creature Voss can see must make a DC 14 WIS save. On a failure, the creature is incapacitated and must move toward the lake on their next turn. Breaks on damage.
Roleplay — First Words
Read aloud: "The bell stops. The fog thickens until it is a wall. Then the water breaks. He rises slowly — robes black with a century of lake mud, face gaunt and hollow-eyed, one hand still clutching a fraying bell rope. When he speaks, his voice is water and rust: 'You came. No one ever came.'"
What He Wants
To be remembered correctly. He does not want revenge — he wants the truth acknowledged. He will attack if threatened but will pause if someone says his name and speaks the truth of what happened.
Peaceful Resolution Trigger
Reading his journal aloud at the shore, bringing the bell fragment (Sable), or having a character acknowledge the betrayal on behalf of the families stops his aggression. He asks one question: "Did anyone remember me?" The answer determines the ending.
CORVIN & DREN — Trapped Fishermen
AC 10  ·  HP 4 each  ·  Currently incapacitated in trance state in the sunken chapel
State
Both are alive but in a waking trance — eyes open, responsive to pain, but not fully present. Dren (Vesper's partner) has a note in his pocket, partially written. Corvin left the note in the boat.
Rescuing Them
Breaking Voss's hold (peaceful resolution OR reducing him to 0 HP) frees them. They wake immediately. Dren asks for Vesper by name. Corvin asks about Sael. Award the party 50 XP each for the rescue.
What They Know (if rescued and questioned)
The pull in the water. The warmth. The voice repeating what sounds like a word or name. Dren: "It wasn't angry. It was just... waiting." Corvin: "Something under the steeple was turning. Like a wheel. Slow."
VILLAGE NPCs — Minor Characters
Sael — Corvin's wife, runs the dockhouse
Frightened, sharp, holding it together. Will not go near the water. Knows about the note her husband left in the boat. Will trade information for reassurance that Corvin is alive.
Old Pell — Elder fisherman, 80s, doesn't fish anymore
Remembers when the steeple was still visible above the waterline, fifty years ago. Knows where the chapel sits. Will mark it on a rough map if asked. Has no idea why the bell rings.
Callowell Descendants — 3–4 adults, avoid the party
The Callowell family still lives in Mirefall. They know something. DC 15 Persuasion or DC 12 Intimidation to get them talking. They will confirm the land dispute but claim not to know what happened during the flood. Insight DC 13: they're lying.
ENCOUNTER QUICK REFERENCE
Three Possible Endings
1. Peaceful Rest — Party learns the truth, acknowledges it to Voss, returns bell fragment. Voss releases Corvin and Dren, the bell stops, he dissolves into the lake at peace. Marta rewards full coin + shortsword.

2. Forced Banishment — Party defeats Voss in combat without the truth coming out. Bell stops temporarily. He will return. Marta pays the reward but something feels unresolved. Marta's reaction: hollow relief.

3. Voss Ascends — Party sides with Voss, helps him confront the Callowell family directly. Chaotic outcome. Callowell family is terrified; party must mediate a confession. High drama, multiple rolls, Voss finally rests when the confession is made aloud at the shore.
