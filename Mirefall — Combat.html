The Drowned Dead
Encounter 1 · Shoreline / Dock · Act 1
"Something breaks the surface. Not a fish. The water around the dock goes still — and then the hands come up."
DM Notes

Setup: 3 Lake Ghouls + 1 Lake Ghast (leader). Ghouls rise from the water's edge as the party investigates the dock or attempts to launch a boat. Start with 2 ghouls, have the ghast and 3rd ghoul emerge on round 2.

Tactics: Ghouls rush and grapple (Paralysis on hit). Ghast hangs back, uses Stench to debuff, then moves in. If 2+ players are paralyzed, the remaining enemies drag one toward the water. Retreat into the lake if below 10 HP.

Tone: These were the missing fishermen's friends. One ghoul is wearing Corvin's jacket. Let the players notice.

The Lights on the Water
Encounter 2 · On the Lake / Rowboat · Act 2
"The fog thickens as you pull away from shore. Then you see them — pale lights beneath the surface, rising slowly, drawn toward the boat."
DM Notes

Setup: 4 Will-o'-Wisps attack while the party is on the water in rowboats. Two rise from below, two drift in from the fog. The party is constrained — anyone knocked unconscious risks going overboard (DC 12 DEX save or fall in).

Tactics: Wisps use Consume Life on downed targets, Incorporeal Movement to pass through the boat, Ephemeral to go invisible and reposition. They avoid melee, hover just out of reach. They're not trying to kill — they're herding the party toward the chapel site.

Twist: If a player uses a light source (torch, spell), the wisps recoil for one round (disadvantage on attacks). If players figure out the wisps are guiding them, not attacking, they can attempt a DC 13 Insight or Arcana check to realize they should follow the lights. Reward this — it gets them to the chapel faster.

Father Aldric Voss
Encounter 3 · Submerged Chapel · Act 2 Boss · CR 6
"He stands at the bell rope. Still ringing, still ringing, even now. He turns. His eyes are the color of deep water. He says: 'You came. I have been waiting for someone to come.'"
DM Notes — Read Before Running

This fight should feel wrong. Voss doesn't attack first. He speaks. He asks why they've come. Give the party at least one full round to attempt dialogue before he becomes hostile — and he only becomes hostile if they attack or refuse to hear him.

Peaceful resolution: If the party has Marta's letter, the church log, or Marta herself (if she followed them), Voss can be reasoned with. DC 15 Persuasion with evidence = he stops fighting and asks to be told the truth. He needs to hear that someone knows what happened. Once he knows — he rings the bell one final time and dissolves.

Combat trigger: If players attack or fail three consecutive dialogue attempts, Voss enters full combat mode. He's not angry — he's grieving. Have him say "Then I will ring until someone hears" as he attacks.

Lair: Submerged chapel, 20 ft. underwater. Dim light (bioluminescent algae). Difficult terrain (water resistance to movement unless swimming). Bell platform is 10 ft. above the main floor — Voss starts there.
