☩ Village of Mirefall ☩
Notice of Disturbance & Reward
By Order of the Village Elder — This Fourteenth Day of Harvestmoon

Let it be known to all travelers and persons of sound character:

The village of Mirefall is visited by an affliction of unnatural origin. For nine days and nine nights without interruption, a tolling has risen from the depths of our lake — steady, low, and without ceasing. It is heard in sleep. It is felt in the chest. Our fishermen will not put boats out. Our children will not near the shore. Two men who ventured onto the water seven days past have not returned.

50 Gold
Reward — Coin of the Realm

…is offered to any party willing to investigate the source of this disturbance and bring it to an end by whatever means prove necessary. Payment rendered in full upon resolution, to be witnessed by the Elder.

Inquire at the Elder's cottage on the north end of the village road. Ask for Marta.

All those who enter upon the lake do so of their own accord. The village of Mirefall accepts no responsibility for misfortune sustained thereon.

By my hand and seal, Marta Voss Village Elder, Mirefall
— ✦ —
DM Note

This is what drew the party to Mirefall. Marta wrote it in desperation — she knows exactly why the bell is ringing and has for her entire life. The disclaimer at the bottom was added by her hands shaking.

The last entry is the key reveal. It wasn't the water that erased it.

Found: hidden beneath the floorboard in Marta's cottage — Investigation DC 15
To Whoever Comes After Me —

I have kept this secret for sixty-one years. I learned it from my mother, who learned it from hers, who was a girl of eight when the flood came and saw everything from the hillside.

Father Voss rang the chapel bell to warn the village. He had seen the dam failing and ran to the church. He rang for an hour before the water took him.

The Callowell family knew. The Brennish family knew. The Vorne Ashvale — four families knew the dam had broken and chose not to send word to the chapel because they were feuding with him over land. They watched from high ground. They watched him ring until he drowned.

Afterward they told the village the bell had rung on its own. A miracle, they said. A warning from God. And everyone chose to believe them because the alternative was too terrible.

My great-grandfather died ringing a bell to save people who let him drown for spite.

I have not told anyone. I do not know how to tell anyone. I am writing this down because the bell has started again and I think — I think he knows I know. I think he has always known.

If someone finds this: his name was Aldric. He was trying to save them. Please let him rest.

— Marta Voss, Elder of Mirefall written in the ninth year of my eldership
DM Note

This is the heart of the mystery. Marta will not volunteer this information — she has carried the guilt of silence her whole life. If players find the letter first, she breaks down completely when confronted. The crossed-out family names are intentional: she started to write them and couldn't. A DC 12 Investigation can partially make out "Vorne" — one of the PC surnames if Thessaly is in the party.

☩ Office of the Village Council — Mirefall ☩
Memorandum of Land Dispute
Regarding the Eastern Orchard Boundary — Filed 2nd Harvestmoon

The following families have filed formal objection to the boundary survey conducted by the Chapel of the Drowned Light, asserting that approximately four acres of eastern orchard land recorded in the chapel's name were improperly transferred in the year of the founding, without consent of the original farming families:

Petitioner 1House Callowell — orchard & northern field
Petitioner 2House Brennish — eastern lower pasture
Petitioner 3House Drave — disputed access road
Petitioner 4House Vorne — southern quarter-acre, mill rights
Petitioner 5House Ashvale — retracted 8th Harvestmoon
Petitioner 6House — retracted prior to filing

The matter was submitted to Father Aldric Voss for response. His reply, received 9th Harvestmoon, stated that the boundary records were accurate and that he would "make no concession on land that belongs to God's house."

Resolution of this dispute is pending council review, scheduled for the 16th — never convened.

Council seal affixed. Filed in perpetuity.

— ✦ —
DM Note

This document establishes motive. Note the sixth petitioner is blotted out — that family quietly withdrew and then, separately, warned the village during the flood while the others did not. The Ashvale retraction is also notable: House Ashvale stepped back from the feud just before the flood. If Oryn Ashvale is in the party, this lands hard.

BACKSTORY
Oryn grew up in a family that never spoke of the past. Their grandmother, a sharp-eyed woman named Hessa, spent her final years sleeping restlessly and waking from dreams she refused to describe. BACKSTORY
Vesper Crane has worked the dark roads as a patrol ranger for six years. They are good at silence, good at waiting, and good at tracking things that don't want to be found. Their partner Dren was the opposite — loud, warm, always talking. They balanced each other out. Vesper has been following Dren's trail ever since, sleeping in three-hour stretches, eating when they remember to. Vesper came to Mirefall not for the reward, not for answers. They came because Dren is still out there, and they do not leave partners behind.

BACKSTORY
Sable doesn't remember picking up the piece of iron. One day it was in the lining of their pack — old, corroded, curved, clearly a fragment of something larger. A blacksmith identified it as bell metal. Ancient bell metal. For six months before arriving at Mirefall, Sable had been having the same dream: underwater, cold, a tremendous ringing from all sides, and a voice saying "Bring it back." The fragment grows warmer the closer they get to the lake. Sable took the Oath of Vengeance because the world is full of things that refuse to be held accountable. But Father Voss is not one of those things — he was the victim. This is the first situation in Sable's career as a paladin where the target of a sworn oath turned out to be the wronged party. They have not figured out what that means for them yet.
PERSONALITY

FLAW

BACKSTORY
Brother Mael has spent twenty years learning to be invisible, to be silent, to be useful in the dark. The monastery asks few questions and answers fewer. When the abbot gave Mael a sealed letter addressed to Father Aldric Voss at Mirefall Lake and said only that it was a matter of great spiritual importance, Mael accepted the assignment without asking for details.
